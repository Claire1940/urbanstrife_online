<Section icon={Swords} title="Tactical Combat & Action Point Mechanics">

Once your performance is optimized, mastering the tactical combat engine is your next step. Urban Strife utilizes a highly detailed Action Point (AP) system reminiscent of classic tactical RPGs like Phoenix Point. Unlike modern XCOM games which limit you to two simple moves, characters here possess a deep pool of 30 to 40 AP to allocate dynamically.

### Combat Actions and AP Costs

Every physical movement, weapon interaction, and tactical maneuver consumes a portion of your turn's AP pool. Planning your actions efficiently is the difference between survival and a swift wipe:

| Tactical Action | Typical AP Cost | Strategic Value |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Basic Movement** | 1-2 AP per tile | Repositioning behind cover or flanking enemies. |
| **Crouch / Stand** | 3-5 AP | Increases accuracy and defense; limits mobility. |
| **Swap Weapon** | 5 AP | Adapting to range changes (e.g., switching to melee). |
| **Quick Shot** | 10-15 AP | Low-accuracy attack; useful for finishing weak targets. |
| **Aimed Shot** | 20-25 AP | High accuracy; allows targeting specific body parts. |
| **Reload Weapon** | 8-12 AP | Essential upkeep; always reload before initiating combat. |

Combat transitions seamlessly from real-time exploration to turn-based tactical grids once an enemy spots your squad. During combat, players can hold **Shift and Left-Click** on an enemy to target individual body parts (such as the head, torso, or limbs). 

However, the UI does not display a simple percentage-to-hit indicator. Instead, it relies on range feedback (such as "Optimal" or "Far"). Green, blue, and gray health bars represent health and stamina metrics, which require careful observation.

<Callout type="warning" title="Tactical Warning">
Urban Strife does not feature an "Undo" button. A single misclick can cause your character to run directly into the open, leaving them completely exposed to enemy gunfire. Because the game is unforgiving even on lower difficulties, save your progress manually before every major skirmish.
</Callout>

</Section>

Outside of combat, your primary loop involves managing your base of operations: a sprawling mansion housing survivors. This base management system functions similarly to State of Decay, requiring you to monitor daily consumption rates for vital resources like food, fuel, materials, and overall morale.

### Base Resource Management

Keeping your shelter running smoothly requires constant scouting and efficient inventory management:

| Resource Type | Daily Consumption | Primary Source | Consequences of Depletion |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Food** | High | Kitchen, scavenging | Severe morale drop, starvation injuries. |
| **Fuel** | Medium | Gas stations, industrial zones | Power outages, disabled base upgrades. |
| **Meds** | Low (unless injured) | Pharmacies, hospitals | Untreated infections, permanent death of survivors. |
| **Materials** | Dynamic (construction) | Hardware stores, debris | Inability to build defenses or repair gear. |
