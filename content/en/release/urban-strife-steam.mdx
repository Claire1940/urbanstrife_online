<Section icon={Swords} title="Tactical Combat & Controls">

Combat in Urban Strife is governed by a turn-based Action Point system. Unlike many other tactical RPGs, this game simulates bullet trajectories, material penetration, and individual explosion fragments. Positioning is not just about "hit chance" but about the physical properties of the world around you.

### Essential Combat Shortcuts

| Key | Action | Tactical Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **X** | Swap Active Weapon | Switch between ranged and melee quickly |
| **F** | Change Fire Mode | Toggle between single, burst, or auto |
| **Y** | Activate Overwatch | Set up reaction fire for the enemy turn |
| **0** | Select Entire Party | Useful for group positioning outside combat |
| **C** | Change Stance | Toggle between standing, crouched, and prone |

<Callout type="warning" title="Cover Material Matters">
Not all cover is equal. A wooden fence might provide a visual "hit penalty" to enemies, but a high-caliber rifle will penetrate it easily. Always prioritize stone walls, engine blocks, or thick concrete for maximum protection.
</Callout>

### Advanced Tactical Mechanics

1. **Initiative**: Entering combat while crouched and in cover increases your chance of winning initiative, allowing your squad to act first.
2. **Body-Part Targeting**: Headshots deal massive damage but have lower accuracy. Use limb shots to slow down fast-moving "runners" or disable human marksmen.
3. **Noise & Awareness**: Every action generates noise. Explosions and unsuppressed shotguns can pull zombies from adjacent sectors into your current battle.
4. **Interrupt Fire**: Characters with high Agility and remaining AP can interrupt enemy movement, providing a crucial layer of defense during the enemy turn.

<Callout type="info" title="Stealth Advantage">
You can resolve many encounters without entering full turn-based combat. If you remain undetected, you can thin out enemy ranks using silent takedowns, saving your ammunition for the final objectives.
</Callout>

</Section>
