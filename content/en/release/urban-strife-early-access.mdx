<Section icon={Swords} title="Tactical Combat and Realistic Ballistics">

Combat in Urban Strife is governed by a classic Action Point (AP) system, but it distinguishes itself through an advanced ballistics engine. Unlike many RPGs that rely on abstract hit rolls, this game calculates the trajectory of every individual projectile. This includes shotgun pellets and grenade fragments, making positioning and cover choices far more impactful than simple percentage buffs.

<CardGrid cols={3}>
  <Card title="Ranged Precision">
    - **Body Part Targeting**: Cripple limbs or go for risky headshots.
    - **Bullet Penetration**: High-caliber rounds can shoot through thin walls.
    - **Attachment Synergy**: Use scopes and compensators to offset recoil.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Melee & Stealth">
    - **Silent Takedowns**: Neutralize enemies without alerting the map.
    - **Improvised Weapons**: Use anything from frying pans to spiked bats.
    - **Stamina Management**: Melee requires careful AP and energy balance.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Horde Tactics">
    - **Overwatch Ambushes**: Set up kill zones for approaching undead.
    - **Simultaneous Turns**: Watch the entire horde move in one phase.
    - **Environmental Kills**: Use explosive barrels or collapsing structures.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

The ballistics simulation means that the material of your cover matters. A wooden fence might stop a pistol round but will be shredded by a hunting rifle. Similarly, hiding behind a car engine block provides significantly more protection than hiding behind the car door.

<Callout type="tip" title="Combat Strategy">
Always check the "Chance-to-Hit" calculator before firing. It doesn't just show a percentage; it estimates potential damage based on bullet attenuation and the specific body part targeted.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Shelter Management and Survival Mechanics">

The Shelter is the heart of your operations. When you first arrive, it is a crumbling ruin overcrowded with frail refugees. Your success depends on your ability to scavenge materials and staff the base with specialists like engineers, chefs, and pharmacists.

Scavenging is a high-stakes loop. You must balance the need for food and medicine with the risk of attracting a zombie horde or a rival gang. Once a siege begins, all scavenging stops, and you must survive solely on the supplies you have stockpiled and the defenses you have built.
