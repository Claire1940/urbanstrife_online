<Section icon={Target} title="Beginner Guide: Surviving the First Hours">

Surviving the early hours of the **urban strife full release** requires careful resource management and a solid understanding of exploration loops. Do not treat every encounter as an open gunfight. Ammo and medicine are scarce, and early mistakes will cripple your progression.

<Callout type="warning" title="Ammunition and Medicine Warning">
Firing unsuppressed weapons attracts nearby wandering zombies. Always attempt to resolve isolated encounters silently using melee weapons or crossbows to conserve precious ammunition.
</Callout>

<Steps>

  <Step num="2" title="Inspect Urban Shelter">

  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Assemble a Balanced Away Team">

<Section icon={Swords} title="Tactical Combat, Overwatch, and Controls">

Combat in Urban Strife is turn-based, driven by simulated bullet trajectories, material penetration, and dynamic noise ranges. Understanding how to manipulate Action Points (AP) and cover materials is the difference between an easy victory and a squad wipe.

<CardGrid cols={3}>
  <Card title="AP Management">
    Every step, reload, and attack costs Action Points. Always preview your movement destination to ensure you retain enough AP to fire or enter Overwatch.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Material Penetration">
    Cover is destructible and penetrable. A car engine block stops high-caliber rounds, whereas thin wooden fences can be easily shot through by hunting rifles.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Stealth and Noise">
    Dynamic vision cones and sound waves dictate enemy awareness. Crouching behind low walls reduces your detection profile, allowing for high-damage stealth ambushes.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

<Callout type="info" title="Combat Controls and Shortcuts">
Use these essential keyboard shortcuts to streamline tactical combat:
- Press **X** to swap between your active primary and secondary weapons.
- Press **F** to cycle through your weapon's available fire modes (Single, Burst, Auto).
- Press **Y** to instantly set up an Overwatch cone in a specified direction.
- Press **0** to select the entire squad for group movement outside of combat.
</Callout>

When facing human opponents, flank their positions to bypass their cover bonuses. When dealing with zombie hordes, retreat to narrow chokepoints and utilize shotguns, automatic fire, and Molotov cocktails to manage the crowd before they surround your fighters.

</Section>

<Section icon={Hammer} title="Shelter Upgrades and Crafting Progression">

Rebuilding the Urban Shelter is vital to surviving the campaign's ultimate challenge. Your base upgrades, power grid, and resource stockpiles directly feed into the **Defense Tracker**, which determines your survival odds during the final siege.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Stabilize Daily Needs">
    Prioritize basic survival. Establish a steady supply of food, clean water, and medicine. Keep generators fueled to maintain basic power to your storage facilities.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Restore Town Services">
    Reconstruct core facilities in the town center. Repair the City Radio to unlock new questlines, the Hospital for passive recovery, and the Workshop for crafting.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Establish Food Industries">
    Build Gardens, Hydroponics, and Rat Farms. Generating food internally ensures your population does not starve when late-game roaming hordes cut off scavenging routes.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Upgrade the Workshop">
    Retool your workshop to unlock advanced crafting stations. This allows you to manufacture custom attachments, specialty ammunition (dum-dum, armor-piercing), and explosives.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Fortify and Power Up">
    Construct defensive walls and solar or wind generators. Link your powered defenses (like electric fences) to a stable power grid to prepare for the final siege.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Ballistic Plate Replacement">
While standard body armor and helmets can be fully restored using spare parts at Owen's repair station, internal ballistic plates are consumable. Once destroyed, they must be discarded and replaced with newly crafted or looted plates.
</Callout>
