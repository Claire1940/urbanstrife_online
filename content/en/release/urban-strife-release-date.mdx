<Section icon={Target} title="Beginner Guide: Surviving the First 24 Hours">

The opening hours of Urban Strife are designed to teach you the nuances of tactical movement and resource conservation. After the initial prologue at the bridge, your primary goal is to stabilize the Urban Shelter. You must balance the immediate needs of the residents—food, water, and morale—with the long-term necessity of upgrading facilities.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Complete the Prologue">
    Follow the CDC evacuation storyline. This sequence introduces basic equipment slots, inventory management, and the "Interrupt Fire" mechanic. Do not rush; use this time to familiarize yourself with the UI.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Stabilize Shelter Resources">
    Once you arrive at the Shelter, prioritize the donation flow. Return from your first few missions with food, medicine, and fuel. These are essential for keeping the hospital and radio operational.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Assemble a Balanced Team">
    Do not rely on a single "super-soldier." Distribute weapons based on ammo availability. An ideal early team consists of one quiet melee specialist, one marksman with a hunting rifle, and one support character carrying medical supplies.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Establish Stealth Protocols">
    Ammunition is scarce. Use crossbows or melee takedowns on isolated zombies. Gunfire attracts more enemies and can quickly lead to your squad being surrounded by a roaming horde.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Looting Strategy">
Avoid "looting everything" in the first map. Carrying too much weight reduces your Action Points in combat. Focus on high-value materials like chemicals and electronics for early Shelter upgrades.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Tactical Combat and Weapon Hierarchy">

Combat in Urban Strife is governed by a sophisticated ballistics engine. Every bullet is a physical object that can penetrate materials or fragment upon impact. Understanding the relationship between cover material and weapon caliber is the difference between life and death. For instance, a wooden fence might stop a pistol round but will offer no protection against a .308 hunting rifle.

| Weapon Role | Best For | Key Strength | Limitation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Shotguns** | Horde Control | High close-range stopping power | Rapid ammo consumption |
| **Rifles** | Human Snipers | Excellent penetration and range | High AP cost per shot |
| **Crossbows** | Stealth Kills | Completely silent; reusable bolts | Slow reload speed |
| **Pistols** | Sidearms | Low AP cost for finishing moves | Poor cover penetration |
| **Explosives** | Chokepoints | Area denial and fragment damage | Extremely loud; attracts hordes |

<Callout type="info" title="Overwatch and Interrupts">
Always save a few Action Points at the end of your turn to trigger **Overwatch**. This allows your survivors to fire automatically during the enemy's turn if they move into your line of sight. This is especially effective in narrow hallways or doorways.
</Callout>

</Section>

There are three primary power blocs in Urban County: the **National Guard** (Rogue Army), the **Church of Second Chance**, and the **Shady Lady Bikers**.
