<Section icon={Users} title="Recruiting Urban Strife Companions: Building Your Militia">

In the post-apocalyptic world of Urban Strife, your survival depends entirely on the quality and composition of your squad. **Urban strife companions** are not just generic units; they are individual survivors with backstories, native perks, and unique defining traits that influence both combat performance and dialogue options. Following the 1.0 release on July 14, 2026, the recruitment system has been expanded to include more than twenty ally upgrades and a deeper reputation system that dictates who will join your cause.

Recruitment begins at the Urban Shelter, but your roster will quickly grow as you explore Urban County. You will encounter independent survivors, refugees, and faction-aligned soldiers who can be convinced to join your militia through successful dialogue checks, completing specific interests, or securing enough political votes within the county.

| **Independent Survivors** | Rescue missions / Dialogue | Early-game specialists, Medics |
| **National Guard** | High Reputation / Quests | Military-grade shooters, Armored units |

<Section icon={Target} title="Character Attributes and Learn-by-Doing Progression">

Urban Strife utilizes a realistic "learn-by-doing" progression system. Unlike traditional RPGs where you assign points upon leveling up, your **urban strife companions** improve their attributes by performing specific actions in the field. If a survivor frequently uses a sniper rifle, their Marksmanship will increase. If they spend their turns sneaking through the shadows, their Agility will grow.

This system encourages specializing your survivors into dedicated roles. Intelligence plays a critical role here, as it determines the speed at which a character develops all other attributes. A survivor with high Intelligence will master their profession much faster than a less capable recruit.

| Attribute | Primary Effect | Growth Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Marksmanship** | Ranged accuracy / Critical chance | Firing firearms, Crossbow use |
| **Agility** | AP for movement / Evasion / Interrupts | Sneaking, Dodging melee attacks |
| **Dexterity** | Item use AP cost / Melee accuracy | Using tools, Medkits, Melee combat |
| **Strength** | Carry weight / Melee damage / Stamina | Melee kills, Carrying heavy loads |
| **Endurance** | Max Health / Stamina / Injury resistance | Taking damage, Long expeditions |
| **Intelligence** | Attribute growth speed / Dialogue checks | Successful skill use, Reading manuals |

<Callout type="warning" title="AP Management">
Every action, from moving to reloading, consumes Action Points (AP). Always preview your destination to ensure your companion has enough AP remaining to take cover or prepare an Overwatch shot.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Faction Alliances and Unique Recruit Rewards">

Your choice of alliance significantly impacts which **urban strife companions** reach their full potential. The three major blocs—the National Guard, the Shady Lady Bikers, and the Cult of Second Chance—each offer unique blueprints, equipment, and specialist recruits that are unavailable to their enemies.

By Day 20, you must have secured enough votes to enter a faction's headquarters and finalize an alliance. This alliance unlocks a dedicated upgrade branch in your Shelter, providing permanent buffs to your militia and access to the best gear in the game.

| Faction | Unique Recruit Benefit | Specialty Equipment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **National Guard** | Military discipline / High Marksmanship | .50 BMG Rifles, Army HMMWV, Body Armor |
| **Shady Lady Bikers** | Mechanic skills / Vehicle support | Black-market weapons, Vehicle parts |
| **Cult of Second Chance** | Stealth affinity / Unique traits | Specialist crossbows, Hidden blueprints |
| **Local Barons** | Regional knowledge / Trade buffs | Quest rewards, Additional refugees |

<Callout type="info" title="The Defense Tracker">
Allies don't just provide recruits; they contribute to your Defense Tracker. A strong alliance can mean the difference between a successful defense of the Urban Shelter and a catastrophic failure during the final siege.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Map} title="Step-by-Step Guide to Strengthening Your Squad">

Building a powerful team of **urban strife companions** is a gradual process that involves exploration, resource management, and tactical combat. Follow these steps to ensure your militia is ready for the endgame.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Complete the Prologue">
    Focus on the "Patient from Atlanta" storyline. Learn the basics of AP, inventory management, and destroying zombie spawners. Secure your first few recruits from the aging residents of the Shelter.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Restore Essential Services">
    Prioritize construction materials to repair the Hospital, Workshop, and Barracks. You cannot support a large team of companions if you lack the beds to house them or the medical facilities to treat their wounds.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Specialize Your Away Team">
    Identify the natural strengths of your recruits. Assign one as a dedicated medic, another as a long-range marksman, and at least one as a melee specialist to conserve ammunition during quiet encounters.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Farm Reputation and Votes">
    Travel to distant sectors to meet faction leaders. Complete their interests to earn the votes required for headquarters access. Use Intelligence and Strength checks to resolve conflicts peacefully when possible.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Finalize Your Alliance">
    Enter your chosen faction's HQ. Accept the alliance to unlock Tier 3 equipment and faction-specific Shelter upgrades. This is the only way to obtain the most powerful weapons for your companions.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Equipment Durability">
Weapons and armor decay with use. Use the Version 1.0 repair system at Owen's workshop to restore your best gear. Remember that while body armor can be fixed, ballistic plates are expendable and must be replaced.
</Callout>

</Section>
