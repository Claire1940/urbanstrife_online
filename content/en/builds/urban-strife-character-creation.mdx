<Section icon={UserPlus} title="Understanding Core Attributes">

In **urban strife character creation**, your initial attribute allocation sets the foundation for your survivor's entire journey. Unlike traditional RPGs where stats remain static, Urban Strife utilizes a dynamic growth system where repeated actions improve specific attributes. However, your starting points determine your "floor" and how quickly you can reach elite performance levels in the early hours of the 1.0 release.

The game features six primary attributes that influence everything from your carrying capacity to your ability to interrupt enemy turns. Understanding the synergy between these stats is the difference between a survivor who thrives in the Atlanta Horde siege and one who falls in the first suburban skirmish.

| Attribute | Primary Combat Effect | Non-Combat Utility | Growth Trigger |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Strength** | Melee damage / Stamina | **Carry capacity** / Intimidation | Melee hits / Heavy lifting |
| **Dexterity** | Melee hit chance / AP cost | **Crafting** / Tool efficiency | Using items / Melee combat |
| **Agility** | Movement AP / Evasion | Stealth / Interrupt chance | Sneaking / Dodging attacks |
| **Endurance** | Max Health / Stamina | Injury resistance | Taking damage / Long marches |
| **Intelligence** | Attribute growth speed | **Dialogue checks** / Tactics | Successful skill use |
| **Marksmanship** | Ranged accuracy | Weapon maintenance | Landing ranged shots |

<Callout type="tip" title="Strategic Allocation">
Prioritize **Intelligence** if you want a character that scales quickly. High Intelligence increases the progression speed of all other attributes, making it the most valuable long-term investment for a "main" character.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Creation Process">

Creating your leader in Urban Strife requires balancing immediate survival needs with long-term specialization. The 1.0 version introduced a refined UI that highlights how your biography choices affect your starting native perks. Follow this sequence to ensure your character is optimized for the challenges of Urban County.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Define Your Biography">
    Select a background that provides a **Native Perk** aligned with your preferred playstyle. Backgrounds like "Field Experience" or "Barter" provide permanent bonuses that cannot be acquired elsewhere. Your biography also influences how certain factions, like the Shady Lady Bikers, initially perceive you.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Distribute Attribute Points">
    Allocate your limited starting points. For a balanced starter, aim for at least 6 points in **Intelligence** to boost growth and 7 points in your primary combat stat (Marksmanship for shooters, Strength for melee). Avoid "dumping" Endurance, as low health makes the early game extremely punishing.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Select Initial Profession Perks">
    Profession perks are categorized into three tiers. In the creation screen, you will choose your Tier 1 perk. These perks define your role, such as a "Medic" for healing efficiency or a "Scout" for improved vision and stealth.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Finalize Appearance and Gear">
    While cosmetic, your starting gear is determined by your background. Some backgrounds start with better melee tools, while others might provide a basic firearm with limited ammunition. Ensure your starting quick-slots are configured for immediate utility.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Irreversible Choices">
Native perks and defining traits are **permanent**. Unlike profession perks, which can be expanded as you level up, these base traits will stay with your character until the end of the campaign. Choose a background that offers utility you cannot easily find in other recruits.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Profession Perks and Skill Tiers">

The profession perk system in Urban Strife allows for deep specialization. As your character performs tasks, they unlock access to higher tiers of perks. This system ensures that a character who spends their time sniping will naturally unlock perks that enhance long-range lethality, while a dedicated scavenger will become more efficient at resource recovery.

| Perk Tier | Unlock Requirement | Impact Level | Example Specializations |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Tier 1** | Initial Creation / Level 1 | **Foundational** | Basic Medic, Scout, Gunner |
| **Tier 2** | Mid-game progression | **Specialized** | Combat Surgeon, Marksman, Brawler |
| **Tier 3** | Late-game expertise | **Elite** | Field Legend, Ghost, Siege Master |

When planning your **urban strife character creation** strategy, look at the Tier 3 perks early. If you want to be the ultimate sniper, you must ensure your Agility and Marksmanship are high enough to support the requirements of those elite-tier skills later in the campaign.

<Callout type="success" title="Synergy Bonus">
Matching your weapon type to your profession perk provides cumulative bonuses. For example, using a shotgun with a "Horde Control" focused perk tree increases your pellet spread efficiency and suppression capabilities significantly.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Survival and Team Integration">

Your created character does not fight alone. Throughout the campaign, you will recruit survivors with their own unique backgrounds and skills. Effective **urban strife character creation** involves planning for a balanced four-person away team. If you create a pure combat character, you must prioritize recruiting a dedicated medic and a mechanic early on.

| Squad Role | Essential Attribute | Key Responsibility | Early Recruit Match |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Lead Scout** | Agility | Stealth, scouting, and traps | High Agility survivors |
| **Combat Medic** | Dexterity | Healing and status removal | Intelligence-based recruits |
| **Heavy Gunner** | Strength | Suppression and horde control | High Endurance survivors |
| **Tech Expert** | Intelligence | Crafting and lockpicking | High Dexterity survivors |

<Callout type="warning" title="Inventory Management">
Avoid giving all your best gear to your main character. Distributing weapons and medicine across the squad ensures that if your leader is suppressed or injured, the rest of the team can still function effectively.
</Callout>
