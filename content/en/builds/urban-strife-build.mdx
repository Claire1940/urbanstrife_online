The attribute system dictates your Action Points (AP), which are the lifeblood of tactical encounters. High AP allows for multiple shots or repositioning after an attack, which is vital when facing the "Swarm AI" that moves zombies simultaneously.

| Attribute | Primary Impact | Tactical Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Strength** | Carry weight, melee damage | Increased health, better recoil control |

| **Intelligence** | Skill point gain, crafting | Faster facility upgrades, better loot |
| **Constitution** | Maximum health, stamina | Resistance to injury, fatigue recovery |

<Section icon={Zap} title="The Ballistic System and Tactical Depth">

One of the most innovative features affecting your **Urban Strife build** is the ballistic system. Every projectile's path is calculated in real-time, taking into account the material of the objects it passes through. This means your tactical choices must go beyond just "hitting the target." You need to consider the environment as a weapon or a shield.

| Material Type | Penetration Difficulty | Tactical Advice |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Wood/Drywall** | Low | Use high-caliber rounds to kill enemies behind cover. |
| **Brick/Concrete** | Medium | Use as primary cover; avoid light-caliber fire. |
| **Metal/Steel** | High | Best protection; requires armor-piercing rounds. |
| **Glass** | Very Low | Provides visibility but zero protection from fire. |

Your build should account for the types of enemies you face. Against the Rogue Army, you need high-penetration builds. Against the Cult of Second Chance, who often lack heavy armor but use numbers, high-AP builds with AOE (Area of Effect) weapons are more effective.

<Section icon={Wrench} title="Base Integration and Resource Management">

Your **Urban Strife build** is only as strong as the community supporting it. The Urban Shelter serves as your primary hub for upgrading facilities and crafting the gear necessary for high-level encounters. Resource management is a critical pillar of survival; you must balance the morale of your group with the need for better equipment.

| Facility | Build Benefit | Key Resources |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Workshop** | Craft specialized ammo and attachments | Scrap Metal, Tools, Electronics |
| **Garden** | Sustain food supplies and morale | Seeds, Water, Fertilizer |
| **Barracks** | Increase recruitment cap and recovery | Bedding, Meds, Comfort items |
| **Laboratory** | Create advanced medical supplies | Chemicals, Glassware, Fuel |

Crafting allows for "Custom Creations." A survivor with high Intelligence can unlock unique weapon attachments that significantly reduce AP costs or increase penetration stats. This makes the Intelligence attribute more valuable for long-term survival than it might initially appear.

<Callout type="info" title="Shelter Tip">
Don't neglect the Garden. High morale provides passive buffs to all survivors in combat, including increased AP regeneration and better accuracy.
</Callout>
