<Section icon={User} title="Understanding Attributes and Natural Growth">

In Urban Strife, your character build is not just a set of static numbers chosen at a menu screen. Instead, the game utilizes a dynamic growth system where the way you play directly influences how your survivors evolve. Every survivor starts with a unique background, a native perk, and a defining trait that sets their baseline potential.

The six core attributes govern every tactical interaction in the wasteland. Understanding how these interact with your equipment and the environment is the first step in creating an optimized squad.

| Attribute | Primary Effects | Growth Method |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Marksmanship** | Ranged accuracy, crit chance | Firing firearms, crossbows |
| **Agility** | Movement AP, interrupts, evasion | Sneaking, dodging melee attacks |
| **Strength** | Carry weight, melee damage, stamina | Carrying heavy loads, melee hits |
| **Dexterity** | Item AP cost, melee hit chance | Using tools, medical kits, melee |
| **Endurance** | Max health, stamina, resistance | Taking damage, long expeditions |
| **Intelligence** | Attribute growth speed, dialogue | Successful skill checks, exploration |

<Callout type="tip" title="Growth Optimization">
Since Intelligence increases the progression speed of all other attributes, starting a custom character with higher Intelligence can lead to a much stronger late-game build as they will level up their combat skills faster than peers.
</Callout>

### The Learn-by-Doing Mechanic
Unlike traditional RPGs where you distribute points upon leveling up, Urban Strife rewards consistency. If you want a survivor to become a master sniper, they must spend their turns taking long-range shots. If you want a frontline tank, they need to engage in melee and withstand pressure. This organic growth means your "build" is a reflection of your tactical history.

</Section>

To maximize a shooter's efficiency, you must manage their Action Points (AP) carefully. A high-Agility build allows for repositioning and firing in the same turn, which is critical when the Atlanta Horde begins to close in.

<Section icon={Swords} title="Melee and Stealth: The Silent Killers">

Ammo is a luxury in Urban County. A dedicated melee build is not just a backup; it is a necessity for clearing sectors quietly and conserving resources. Stealth specialists take this a step further by utilizing the game's dynamic sight and hearing ranges to dismantle enemy camps without triggering a full-scale firefight.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Attribute Synchronization">
    Focus on **Strength** for raw damage and **Dexterity** to lower the AP cost of attacks. A high-Dexterity melee fighter can often strike twice in the time a heavy hitter strikes once.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Stealth Conditioning">
    Level up **Agility** by sneaking through zombie-infested areas. Avoid running, as noise will attract roaming hordes that can overwhelm a lone scout.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Weapon Selection">
    Use blades or piercing weapons for silent kills. Blunt weapons are excellent for damaging enemy stamina, which can lead to non-lethal knockouts—useful when dealing with certain faction NPCs you might want to recruit later.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Melee Build Efficiency Comparison:**
| Weapon Class | AP Cost | Noise Level | Armor Penetration |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Combat Knives** | Low | **Silent** | Low |
| **Baseball Bats** | Medium | Low | **Medium** |
| **Heavy Axes** | High | Medium | **High** |
| **Crossbows** | Medium | **Silent** | Medium |

<Callout type="success" title="Stealth Advantage">
A stealth build with high Agility can often trigger **Interrupt Fire** or reaction attacks more frequently. Position these characters near doorways to catch human enemies off-guard during their turn.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Heart} title="Support Roles: Medics and Utility Survivors">

A squad of pure killers will eventually succumb to attrition. The **urban strife character build** for support focuses on Intelligence and Dexterity, ensuring that your team stays healthy and your equipment stays functional.

**The Combat Medic**
Medics shouldn't just sit in the back. A high-Endurance medic can perform rescues under fire. Their growth comes from using medical supplies, so designate one survivor as the primary healer to maximize their efficiency and unlock high-tier medical perks.

**The Utility & Crafting Specialist**
This character build is often the most overlooked but becomes the most important during the final siege. They handle the heavy lifting, tool usage, and complex dialogue checks with factions like the **National Guard** or the **Church of Second Chance**.
