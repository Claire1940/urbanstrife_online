<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Scaling and Survival Lethality">

In Urban Strife, combat is turn-based and highly tactical. On higher **urban strife difficulty** settings, the "heat chance" or hit probability for ranged weapons is less forgiving. You must account for cover, elevation, and the specific body parts you are targeting. Headshots deal massive damage but have a lower success rate, making them a high-risk, high-reward strategy that is often necessary when facing "tanky" zombies or armored human foes.

Friendly fire is a major factor that scales with difficulty. In Hardcore mode, positioning your squad in a line is a recipe for disaster, as your own survivors can accidentally shoot each other for significant damage. This forces a more spread-out, tactical approach to every encounter.

| Action | Noise Level | Ammo Cost | Risk Level | Efficiency |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Crowbar Swing** | Low | None | Medium | ★★★★☆ |
| **Pistol Shot** | Medium | 1 Bullet | High | ★★★☆☆ |
| **Rifle Burst** | High | 3+ Bullets | **Extreme** | ★★★★★ |
| **Stealth Takedown** | **Silent** | None | Low | ★★★★★ |

<Callout type="warning" title="Critical Warning">
Zombies in Urban Strife can occasionally "tank" headshots even on lower difficulties. Never assume a single shot will end a fight; always have a backup plan or enough Action Points (AP) to retreat.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Base Management and Game Over Conditions">

The difficulty isn't just about the zombies you fight; it is about the community you keep. Your base (the Shelter) requires constant upkeep. On Hardcore **urban strife difficulty**, the daily consumption of food and medicine is increased, making every scavenging run a race against time. The game features several "zero-thing" game over conditions that can end your playthrough abruptly.

| Resource | Daily Drain | Consequence of Depletion | Recovery Method |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Food** | High | Starvation / HP Loss | Scavenging / Farming |
| **Medicine** | Moderate | Disease / Permanent Death | Hospital Raids / Crafting |
| **Morale** | Variable | Desertion / Base Mutiny | Luxury Items / Success |
| **Chemicals** | Low | Crafting Stalls | Industrial Looting |

Managing the "residents" of your base is a full-time job. You will have a mix of active combatants and idle survivors. Balancing their needs while sending out teams to find supplies is the core loop of the mid-game. If your morale drops too low, or if you run out of medicine for a critical character, the game does not offer many "second chances."

<Callout type="info" title="Management Insight">
The UI for base management is intentionally plain to mimic a realistic spreadsheet of resources. Pay close attention to the "Starving" and "Sick" indicators in your notebook, as these are the earliest signs of a failing playthrough.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step: Surviving Your First Week on Hardcore">

If you choose to tackle the highest **urban strife difficulty**, you need a specific plan for the first few days. The early game is about minimizing risk and maximizing loot efficiency.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Scavenging">
    Focus on the immediate area around your starting car. Check every trunk and smash windows only when necessary, as the noise can alert nearby scavengers.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Weapon Prioritization">
    Find a crowbar or a spiked roller immediately. Do not rely on firearms until you have a steady supply of at least 50+ rounds of ammunition.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Recruit Lucy & Lee">
    Early characters like Lucy (the sniper) and Lee are essential. Ensure Lucy is positioned on high ground or behind cover to utilize her rifle effectively.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Establish Food Security">
    Your base will consume roughly 20-30 units of food per day. Prioritize grocery stores and kitchens in residential houses during your first three missions.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Strategy Success">
Using a single character for scouting is often safer than moving the whole squad. A lone scout can sneak into houses, identify loot, and retreat without triggering a massive firefight.
</Callout>

</Section>
