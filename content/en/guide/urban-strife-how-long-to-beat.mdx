<Section icon={Swords} title="Tactical Combat and Time Efficiency">

The combat system in Urban Strife is the primary "time sink" for players. Unlike traditional turn-based games where every unit moves individually, this game utilizes a simultaneous movement system for zombies. This means that while you carefully plot your survivors' moves using Action Points (AP), the undead hordes move as a collective, which keeps the pacing tight and prevents the "slog" often found in massive tactical encounters.

The realism of the ballistics also plays a role in how long encounters last. High-caliber rounds can punch through cover, meaning a well-placed shot can end a fight instantly, while poor planning might lead to a prolonged shootout where your team is pinned down.

    - **Ballistic simulation**: Projectiles are blocked by physical objects based on material density.

  <Card title="Zombie Hordes">

<Section icon={Shield} title="Survival Mechanics and Base Management">

The non-combat portion of the game involves managing a ragtag group of survivors. This is where many players struggle, as the resource consumption rate is notoriously high. If you do not scavenge every corner of the map for food, medicine, and morale-boosting items, your camp can quickly descend into anarchy.

Base management is not just a side-activity; it is a ticking clock. If morale drops too low, survivors may turn on each other, leading to a premature end to your campaign. This adds a layer of "pressure" to the **urban strife how long to beat** metric, as you cannot simply wait out the apocalypse—you must constantly be on the move.
