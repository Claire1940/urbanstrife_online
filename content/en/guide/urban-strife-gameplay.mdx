<Section icon={Target} title="Core Mechanics and Tactical Combat">

Understanding the fundamental loop of **urban strife gameplay** is essential for surviving the first week. The game utilizes an Action Point (AP) system common in classic tactical RPGs, but adds layers of environmental interaction and noise physics. Every shot fired and every door smashed generates sound waves that can be detected by enemies across the fog of war. In the 2026 version of the game, these mechanics have been refined to emphasize the "silent but deadly" approach.

Combat is not just about raw damage; it is about positioning and utility. Using the "Overwatch" ability allows your characters to trigger attacks during the enemy turn, which is vital when defending narrow corridors or doorways. Furthermore, the game features a ragdoll physics system and detailed hit locations, making headshots high-risk but high-reward maneuvers.

| Combat Action | AP Cost | Noise Level | Primary Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Melee Attack** | Low | Very Low | Conserves ammo, silent kills |
| **Snap Shot** | Medium | High | Quick defense, lower accuracy |
| **Aimed Shot** | High | High | Increased critical/headshot chance |
| **Overwatch** | Variable | Dependent | Interrupts enemy movement |
| **Reloading** | Low | Low | Necessary for sustained fire |

<Section icon={Shield} title="Base Management and Survivor Needs">

The shelter serves as your hub in **urban strife gameplay**. It is not just a place to rest; it is a living ecosystem that requires constant input. Survivors consume food and medical supplies daily. If the production at the base (currently 5 units per day) does not meet the consumption (30+ units per day), your community will quickly face starvation or disease.

Base progression is tied to "Council Meetings" and your relationship with key NPCs like the Colonel or Kimberly. These interactions unlock new crafting recipes and story milestones. One of the most critical aspects of the 2026 meta is the 20-day countdown to the zombie horde, which forces you to balance short-term survival with long-term fortification.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Character Progression and Skills">

Characters in Urban Strife do not level up through a traditional XP bar. Instead, they improve through practice—a system that rewards specific playstyles. If a character frequently uses melee weapons, their Strength and Melee skills will rise. If they spend their time sneaking through shadows, their Stealth and Agility will see gains.

| Attribute | How to Increase | Gameplay Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Strength** | Carrying heavy loads, Melee | Higher carry weight, melee damage |
| **Agility** | Moving long distances, Sneaking | More AP per turn, lower detection |
| **Dexterity** | Using firearms, Crafting | Higher hit chance, faster reloading |
| **Endurance** | Taking damage, Long missions | Higher HP, resistance to bleeding |

<Section icon={Swords} title="Advanced Survival Tactics">

As you progress toward the 20-day mark, the difficulty spikes. You will encounter better-equipped human enemies and larger "screamer" zombies that can alert the entire map. Mastering advanced tactics like Molotov placement and the use of ballistic tiles for armor becomes a necessity.

The environment is your best weapon. You can destroy cover, set fires to block paths, or use "Overwatch" traps to funnel enemies into kill zones. Always carry at least one "Pipe Bomb" or "Molotov" for emergency crowd control when the undead numbers become overwhelming.

| Weapon Category | Pros | Cons |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Melee (Rolling Pin/Hammer)** | Silent, no ammo needed | High risk, requires proximity |
| **Crossbows** | Silent, recoverable ammo | Slow reload, lower damage |
| **Shotguns** | Massive close-range damage | Extremely loud, heavy ammo |
| **Pistols/Rifles** | Versatile, good range | Requires constant ammo scavenging |

<Callout type="warning" title="Bleeding Status">
Bleeding is the silent killer in **urban strife gameplay**. If a character is bleeding, they lose HP every turn. Always keep bandages in your quick-slots; never wait until after the battle to patch up a wound.
</Callout>
