<Section icon={Swords} title="Understanding the urban strife horde Combat System">

The core of the **urban strife horde** experience lies in its innovative approach to turn-based tactical combat. Unlike traditional RPGs where every individual enemy takes a separate turn, this game implements a dynamic AI system that allows zombies to move simultaneously. This design choice significantly reduces downtime during large-scale encounters, maintaining a high level of tension without the frustration of waiting for dozens of individual units to complete their actions.

<Section icon={Map} title="World Map Events: The Horde on the Move">

Managing the **urban strife horde** on the world map is just as important as the tactical combat itself. Players will frequently encounter "Horde on the Move" events, represented by red icons with crossed swords. These icons indicate a migrating mass of undead heading toward a specific settlement or point of interest. Ignoring these movements can lead to the permanent loss of vital trading hubs and quest locations.

The world map operates on a real-time clock. When a horde icon reaches a location like the Courthouse, a hidden timer begins. If the player does not intervene within this window, the settlement "falls," turning into a permanent spawning ground for the undead. Once a location is lost, all NPCs within are converted into zombies, and the unique resources or quests associated with that area are gone forever.

| Event Type | Map Icon | Primary Objective | Risk Level |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Migrating Horde** | Moving Red Swords | Intercept on the highway before it reaches a camp. | High |
| **Static Siege** | Pulsing Red Icon | Break the perimeter to save trapped survivors. | Extreme |
| **Scavenger Party** | Yellow Search Icon | Defend your scavengers from roaming undead. | Medium |
| **Faction Skirmish** | Blue/Red Swords | Decide whether to intervene or let factions fight. | Variable |

<Callout type="warning" title="Infection Timers">
Simply being present on the map is not enough to stop an infection. If a horde has already reached a settlement, you must actively hunt down every "spawner" and "dead pile" to clear the area. If the event timer expires while you are still inside the map but haven't cleared the enemies, the settlement will still fall.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Case Study: Defending the Courthouse Settlement">

The Courthouse serves as a critical early-game test for handling an **urban strife horde**. Many players struggle with the specific triggers required to "save" this location. As discussed in community circles and confirmed by developers, the Courthouse defense relies on a successful interception on the world map. If the horde reaches the gates, the situation becomes significantly more dire.

To successfully defend the Courthouse, you should follow a specific sequence of actions. Intercepting the horde on the highway is the most efficient method, as it prevents the "siege" state from ever occurring. However, if you are forced to fight within the Courthouse grounds, you must ensure no "dormant spawners" are left behind. A single missed zombie or spawner can trigger a script error that leads to the settlement's destruction despite your best efforts.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Monitor World Map">
    Watch the highway routes leading to the Courthouse for any red "Horde on the Move" icons.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Intercept Early">
    Move your squad to the highway sector and initiate combat before the horde reaches the Courthouse sector.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Clear the Sector">
    During combat, ensure every single zombie is eliminated. Check corners and dark rooms for dormant enemies.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Verify Status">
    After combat, exit to the world map and check if the red icon has disappeared. If the icon remains, re-enter and search for missed spawners.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Developer Insight">
In 2026, patches have addressed rare bugs where dormant spawners wouldn't trigger the "combat end" sequence. If you find yourself stuck, always check the "SaveGames" folder in your AppData to provide logs for the dev team, as they are actively refining the horde/siege mechanics.
</Callout>

</Section>

Facilities within your base provide passive and active bonuses that are essential for long-term survival. For instance, the Workshop allows for the creation of specialized ammunition types, such as hollow-point rounds for unarmored zombies or armor-piercing rounds for dealing with rogue military factions.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Advanced Tactical Tips for Large Encounters">

To truly master the **urban strife horde**, you must look beyond basic shooting. Stealth, environmental manipulation, and the SUV travel system all play roles in your success. The SUV, in particular, acts as a mobile base that allows for expanded travel and extra storage for the massive amounts of loot you'll find in urban ruins.

Material penetration is the most underutilized mechanic by new players. In urban environments, you can often spot a zombie's silhouette through a thin partition. Using a high-caliber rifle, you can take out high-priority targets (like "Screamers" who call more hordes) without ever entering their line of sight.

| Weapon Type | Penetration Power | Ideal Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Shotguns** | Low | Close-range crowd control and clearing rooms. |
| **Assault Rifles** | Medium | Versatile combat and mid-range penetration. |
| **Sniper Rifles** | High | Taking out leaders through walls and cover. |
| **Melee Weapons** | None | Silent kills on isolated stragglers to save ammo. |
