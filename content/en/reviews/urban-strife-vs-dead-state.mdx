<Section icon={Swords} title="Tactical Combat and Stealth Mechanics">

Combat in Urban Strife is unforgiving. Unlike some faster-paced RPGs, every action cost (AP) matters significantly. Players must balance the noise generated by firearms against the efficiency of melee weapons. Stealth is not just a secondary option; it is often a necessity when infiltrating gang-controlled suburbs like Adam's Villa. Crouching reduces visibility and improves accuracy, but it limits mobility.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Base Management and Resource Scavenging">

In the comparison of **urban strife vs dead state**, the base management system in Urban Strife feels more manual. You don't just "have" supplies; you must actively scavenge and then manually donate them to the community stash to meet daily requirements. The shelter consumes food and medical supplies every 24 hours, and failing to meet these quotas can lead to morale drops or character death.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Scavenging">
    Focus on "Base Supplies" like magazines, fine liquor, and canned food in residential areas.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Material Gathering">
    Collect metal scraps, wire, and ceramic tiles to unlock advanced crafting recipes like ballistic tiles.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Manual Donation">
    Return to the shelter and speak with the quartermaster (e.g., Kimberly) to move items from your personal stash to the community pool.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Skill Upgrading">
