<Section icon={Swords} title="Tactical Combat and Advanced Ballistics">

At the heart of the positive **Urban Strife Metacritic** reception is the combat engine. Unlike many RPGs that rely on abstract "hit rolls," Urban Strife utilizes a sophisticated ballistic simulation. Every bullet follows a calculated trajectory, and the material of your cover—whether it is a car engine or a thin wooden fence—determines your survival.

Combat is governed by a classic Action Point (AP) system. However, the "twist" that reviewers frequently mention is the simultaneous zombie movement phase. Instead of watching fifty zombies move one by one, the entire horde acts at once, maintaining a high-tension pace without the tedious wait times common in other turn-based titles.

| Weapon Class | Tactical Role | Ballistic Advantage | Best Usage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Pistols** | Close-range utility | Low AP cost per shot | Finishing wounded enemies |
| **Rifles** | Long-range precision | High penetration power | Shooting through thin walls |
| **Shotguns** | Crowd control | Individual pellet calculation | Clearing tight corridors |
| **Melee** | Silent takedowns | No noise generation | Stealthy zombie clearing |
| **Grenades** | Area denial | Fragment path simulation | Breaking enemy formations |

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Combat Tip">
Always check the material of your cover. A car engine provides near-total protection from small calibers, but a car door can be easily penetrated by high-powered hunting rifles. Use the "Chance-to-Hit" calculator to see how much damage attenuation the cover provides.
</Callout>

</Section>
