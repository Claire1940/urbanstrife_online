Success in the **urban strife courthouse** requires a hybrid approach. While the game allows for loud, aggressive playstyles, the density of the undead in the jail and hospital wings makes stealth the superior choice for the first half of the mission.

When clearing the back rooms, you will encounter zombie spawners. These are organic masses that periodically vomit new enemies into the combat zone. If you do not destroy these with fire or high-explosive damage, the encounter can become an infinite drain on your resources.

<Callout type="warning" title="Detection Warning">
If a zombie "Herder" spots you, they have a wider detection range than standard walkers. They can alert the entire floor, turning a stealth run into a frantic shootout.
</Callout>

| **Herder** | High awareness, alerts others | Silenced ranged fire |
| **Spawner** | Creates new zombies | Molotov/Incendiary |
| **Cultist** | Armed with melee/crossbows | Tactical cover and flanking |

<Section icon={Target} title="The Locked Door Mechanic: Breaking In">

A common point of frustration within the **urban strife courthouse** is the barricaded bathroom door. Many players attempt to use lockpicks or sharp melee weapons (like mower blades or kitchen knives) only to find the door "invulnerable." This is a specific mechanical check implemented by the developers.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Identify the Target">
    Locate the barricaded door where voices are heard. This is usually in the central hospital wing.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Equip a Blunt Weapon">
    Switch to a **Crowbar**, **Hammer**, or even a **Garden Shear** used in blunt mode. Sharp blades do not have the "S" (Special Melee) impact required for wood and metal barricades.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Enter Combat Mode">
    If the cursor does not automatically change, manually enter combat mode. Select the "S" attack mode on your weapon tablet.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Bash the Barricade">
    Expend action points to strike the door. It may take several turns depending on the character's strength and the weapon's durability.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Developer Insight">
In the 2026 build, blunt weapons can also be used to smash car windows for Dexterity XP and looting, though the courthouse doors remain the primary use case for this mechanic.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={MessageSquare} title="Questline: Judge Jenny Saint and the Cult">

The narrative core of the **urban strife courthouse** involves Judge Jenny Saint. She presents herself as the voice of order, but lore clues suggest a darker history. After clearing the undead, you will be granted an audience with her, provided you haven't already turned the courthouse hostile by attacking the guards.

**Key Quest Branches:**
- **The Humanitarian Path**: Attempting to negotiate between the Cult and the Judge. This is difficult and requires high charisma/dialogue checks.
- **The Heel Turn**: Siding entirely with the Judge. This involves eliminating the Cult of Zombie Worshippers and retrieving the Saint estate deeds.
- **The Truth Seeker**: Finding the "Top Secret" research reports that implicate the Judge in the massacre of original hospital patients.

**Inventory Optimization:**
The courthouse is heavy on loot, often leading to encumbrance. Use the "Loot Distribution" strategy:
- **Heavy Items**: Give to Rangtang or Billy Bo.
- **Crafting Ingredients**: Consolidate on Kaye.
- **Medical Supplies**: Keep on Lucy for immediate field use.

<Callout type="warning" title="Construction Materials">
Note that the Courthouse faction rarely sells construction materials (Recycled Goods). For those, you must trade with the Military faction or scavenge the Supermarket.
