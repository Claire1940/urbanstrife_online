<Section icon={Map} title="The Urban Strife Gas Station Dilemma: Cultists vs Bikers">

Navigating the post-apocalyptic wasteland requires constant vigilance and resources, especially when dealing with the pivotal **urban strife gas station** encounters. Early in the game, you will find yourself caught between two conflicting factions at the motel: the local cultists and the rival bikers. The decisions you make here dictate your resource availability, faction reputation, and access to vehicle fuel.

If you choose to side with the cultists at the motel, your primary objective shifts toward sabotaging the local fuel infrastructure. Sergio, one of the prominent figures among the cultists, tasks you with blowing up the nearby gas station to weaken the bikers' hold on the region. Choosing this path alters the local economy and limits your direct fueling options, forcing you to rely on faction traders for your survival needs.

| Faction | Primary Action Required | Immediate Outcome | Fuel Access Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cultists** | Blow up the gas station | Sergio redirects you to Sister Ruby | High prices (120 coins per can) |
| **Bikers** | Defend the gas station | Keep the fuel pumps intact | Standard trading rates |

<Callout type="warning" title="Critical Quest Warning">
Blowing up the gas station permanently removes it as a physical looting location for raw fuel, forcing you to rely entirely on vendor stock or random drops from defeated enemies.
</Callout>

Before initiating the explosion, ensure you have scavenged the surrounding area for loose automobile parts, scrap metal, and medical supplies. Once the demolition is complete, return to Sergio at the motel. He will praise your efforts and promise that the cultists will share their fuel reserves with your squad, directing you to Sister Ruby for your distribution.

Thanks to the RPG script revision patch implemented in 2026, this inventory bug has been resolved. Sister Ruby now correctly stocks gas canisters in her VIP inventory after you complete the motel questline. However, do not expect a massive discount for your destructive efforts.

<Section icon={Swords} title="Vehicle Refueling Mechanics">

Acquiring the police car is a major milestone in your campaign, but keeping it running requires understanding the vehicle interface. A common issue players face is carrying dozens of gas canisters in their inventory or storing them in the car trunk, only to find the vehicle still refuses to start.

The refueling process in this game is manual and requires specific positioning. The game does not automatically pull fuel from your vehicle storage or global camp stash. You must physically interact with the car using your character's active inventory.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Transfer Canister to Active Inventory">
    Move the fuel canister from your vehicle trunk or camp storage directly into the personal inventory of your active character.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Position Your Character">
    Walk your character directly next to the driver's side door or the rear fuel cap of the police car.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Interact via Right-Click">
    Open your inventory, right-click on the gas canister, and select the option to refuel the vehicle from the context menu.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Refueling Efficiency">
Always refuel your vehicle before embarking on long overland journeys. Running out of fuel on the world map triggers emergency random encounters that can catch your squad off guard.
</Callout>

Ensure your active character has sufficient action points and is not in combat mode when attempting to refuel, as performing maintenance during active skirmishes can expose your squad to enemy flanking maneuvers.

</Section>

When planning a scavenging run, prioritize industrial zones, abandoned highway checkpoints, and garage backlots. These areas have the highest probability of yielding intact fuel canisters.
