<Section icon={Settings} title="Understanding Weapon Durability Mechanics">

In the harsh tactical landscape of Urban Strife, your gear is your lifeline. Every shot fired and every blow parried contributes to the gradual degradation of your equipment. Ignoring the condition of your firearms or melee weapons will eventually lead to catastrophic failure at the worst possible moment—usually in the middle of a zombie horde encounter.

Durability is represented by a percentage bar visible in the item's inspection screen. As this bar depletes, the weapon's reliability drops. A firearm at 50% durability has a significantly higher chance of jamming, requiring an action point (AP) cost to clear the malfunction. Once durability hits zero, the weapon becomes "Broken," rendering it unusable until a full restoration is performed at a dedicated facility.

| Condition Level | Durability Range | Combat Effects | Recommended Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Pristine** | 90% - 100% | Full accuracy, No jamming | Regular cleaning |
| **Worn** | 60% - 89% | Standard performance | Monitor status |
| **Damaged** | 30% - 59% | Minor accuracy penalty, Occasional jams | Field repair |
| **Critical** | 1% - 29% | High jam rate, Severe accuracy loss | Immediate repair |
| **Broken** | 0% | Unusable, Cannot be equipped | Workbench restoration |

<Callout type="tip" title="Maintenance Tip">
Always check your squad's gear condition before leaving the shelter. It is much cheaper and faster to maintain a "Worn" weapon than it is to restore a "Broken" one.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Weapon Restoration Process">

If you have a favorite rifle that has finally reached a critical state, follow these steps to ensure a successful restoration. Note that having a character with high technical proficiency will reduce the material cost significantly.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Salvage Spare Parts">
    Collect low-quality or duplicate weapons from the battlefield. Instead of selling them for a few coins, use the "Scrap" option in your inventory to convert them into **Weapon Parts**.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Access the Workbench">
    Return to your base and interact with the Technician's station. Ensure the weapon you wish to fix is unequipped and placed in the global stash or the technician's inventory.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Select Repair Level">
    Choose between a "Quick Fix" (cheaper, lower durability gain) or a "Total Overhaul." A total overhaul requires more time but resets the weapon's condition to 100%.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Assign a Technician">
    Select the squad member with the highest Mechanic or Technician skill. This minimizes the risk of a "botched repair," which could permanently reduce the item's maximum durability.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Technician Synergy">
Characters with the "Gunsmith" trait have a 15% chance to improve the weapon's base stats slightly during a Total Overhaul repair.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Armor Maintenance and Plate Carriers">

Armor in Urban Strife functions differently than weapons. While weapons have a linear degradation, body armor—specifically plate carriers—relies on a modular system. The vest itself provides a base level of protection and utility (like extra ammo slots), but the primary defense comes from the inserted ballistic plates.

When you are hit, the plate absorbs the damage and loses integrity. Once a plate's integrity reaches zero, it is destroyed and no longer provides protection, though the vest remains intact.

| Armor Component | Repair Method | Material Needed | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Cloth/Leather Vest** | Sewing Kit | Cloth / Leather Scraps | Low protection, high mobility |
| **Plate Carrier (Vest)** | Workbench | Metal Scraps / Kevlar | Provides the slots for plates |
| **Steel Plate** | Replacement | New Steel Plate | Heavy, high durability, cheap |
| **Ceramic Plate** | Replacement | New Ceramic Plate | Light, high protection, fragile |

<Callout type="info" title="Plate Carrier Tip">
You may often find high-quality plate carriers that are empty. You must manually drag and drop compatible ballistic plates into the vest's slots in the inventory screen to gain defensive benefits.
</Callout>

</Section>
