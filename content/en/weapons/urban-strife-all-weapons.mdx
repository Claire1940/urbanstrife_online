### Tier S: The Game Changers
Shotguns are the undisputed kings of the apocalypse in Urban Strife. Because the game calculates every pellet separately, a single blast can hit multiple zombies in a cluster, making them the most efficient tool for clearing sectors. Sniper rifles, particularly the rare .50 BMG variants, allow you to bypass enemy cover entirely, shooting through walls to neutralize National Guard soldiers before they can react.

### Tier A: Versatility and Stealth
Crossbows are essential for any "Ironman" run. Since bolts can often be recovered or crafted cheaply, they allow you to save your gunpowder for the big fights. Assault rifles offer the best balance for general exploration, providing burst-fire capabilities for crowd control and single-shot accuracy for human encounters.

<Section icon={Swords} title="Firearms, Ammunition, and Penetration Stats">

The ballistics system in Urban Strife is one of its most complex features. Weapons don't just "hit" or "miss"; they interact with the environment. A thin wooden fence provides almost no protection against a 7.62mm round, whereas a car's engine block can stop most small arms. When exploring the **urban strife all weapons** catalog, pay close attention to the penetration values.

| Weapon Type | Common Calibers | Penetration | Noise Level | AP Cost |

### Ammunition Types
Not all bullets are created equal. Players can find or craft specialized rounds at the Gunsmith Workbench:
- **Hollow Point**: Increased damage against unarmored targets (Zombies), but terrible against Faction soldiers.
- **Armor Piercing**: Essential for fighting the National Guard or Rogue Army Garrison.
- **Dum-Dum Rounds**: Causes bleeding effects, perfect for slowing down high-health "Boss" survivors.

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Weapon Maintenance: Repair and Modification Guide">

The 1.0 update significantly changed how players interact with their gear. You can no longer simply use a weapon forever; durability matters. Once Owen's repair operation is unlocked at the Shelter, you can begin restoring the **urban strife all weapons** you've scavenged to factory condition.

**The Salvage Loop:**
1. Collect duplicate or broken weapons during expeditions.
2. Return to the Shelter and use the Workshop to dismantle them.
3. Use the resulting "Weapon Parts" to repair your high-tier sniper rifles and shotguns.
4. Install modifications like Silencers, Scopes, and Extended Mags to specialize your squad.
