Combat in this **urban strife mission** requires a tactical approach. Use the "Overwatch" mode to set up kill zones. If a scavenger attempts to move through your line of sight during their turn, your characters will automatically fire, provided they have enough AP remaining.

| Action | AP Cost | Tactical Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Crouch** | 4 AP | Increases accuracy and reduces visibility |
| **Overwatch** | Variable | Interrupts enemy movement with auto-fire |
| **Reload** | 6-8 AP | Essential for maintaining fire pressure |
| **Open Door** | 2 AP | Allows for flanking maneuvers inside buildings |

<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Strategy: AP and Cover">

Combat in Urban Strife is unforgiving. A single mistake in positioning can lead to a character bleeding out or being swarmed. The key to winning any **urban strife mission** is mastering the relationship between cover and Action Points. 

Full cover (solid walls) provides the best protection but limits your field of view. Half-cover (crates, low walls) allows for better shooting angles but leaves you vulnerable to flanking.

| Cover Type | Defense Bonus | Accuracy Penalty | Best Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Full Wall** | High | High (must lean) | Reloading and healing |
| **Crates** | Medium | Low | Sustained firefights |
| **Vehicles** | Medium | Medium | Mobile cover during street fights |
| **Open Ground** | None | None | Moving between cover (High Risk) |

**Video Insights on Combat:**
- **Optimal Range**: Every weapon has a "sweet spot" where accuracy is highest.
- **Stances**: Running saves AP but makes noise; crouching costs more AP to move but keeps you hidden.
- **Bleeding**: If a character takes a "red" hit, they are bleeding. You must use a medical kit immediately or they will lose health every turn.

<Callout type="tip" title="The 'Lure' Tactic">
If enemies are entrenched in a building, use a "noise maker" or fire a single shot near the door, then set all your units to Overwatch. Let the scavengers come to you.
</Callout>
