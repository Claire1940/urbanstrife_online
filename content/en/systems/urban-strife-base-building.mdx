<Section icon={Map} title="Base Upgrades & Resource Management">

Upgrading your shelter requires a steady influx of construction materials, electronic parts, and medical supplies. This necessity drives the core looting loop of the game. When exploring sectors, you must systematically search every cabinet, trash can, and corpse. While the looting process can be detailed and requires careful inventory management, the rewards are essential for base progression. You can open two companion inventories simultaneously to transfer items, which helps organize your haul before returning to base.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Secure the Perimeter">
    Clear the immediate surroundings of the shelter to establish a safe zone. This reduces the frequency of minor zombie attacks and allows your survivors to gather basic scrap metal and wood safely.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Establish the Medical Bay">
    Prioritize upgrading the medical facilities. This allows the resident doctor to heal injured squad members faster and reduces the consumption of portable medkits and bandages during recovery.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Construct the Workshop">
    Build a workshop to start crafting custom ammunition, repairing damaged weapons, and creating Molotov cocktails. The workshop is essential for converting raw scrap into usable combat gear.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Reinforce Base Defenses">
    Set up barricades, watchtowers, and choke points around the shelter entrance. These structures slow down incoming hordes and give your defenders a tactical advantage during defensive phases.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Once resources are secured, you must decide which base facilities to construct or upgrade first. The medical bay should always be a high priority, as it allows the base doctor to treat severe wounds and infections without consuming your portable field kits. Upgrading the workshop unlocks the ability to craft advanced ammunition and repair degraded weapons, ensuring your squad remains combat-ready. Balancing these upgrades prevents your shelter from burning through supplies faster than you can salvage them.

<Callout type="success" title="Resource Efficiency">
Upgrading your storage facilities early increases the maximum cap of resources you can hold, preventing waste when returning from high-yield looting runs.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Combat Tactics & Defense Strategies">

Combat in Urban Strife is divided between fighting the mindless undead and battling hostile human factions, such as biker gangs and zombie-worshiping cultists. While zombies are predictable and can be funneled into narrow pathways, human enemies utilize ranged weapons, cover, and tactical flanking. Your base defense strategy must account for both types of threats to ensure the survival of the Coalition.

Zombies are slow, predictable, and highly vulnerable to melee attacks. By positioning your squad at narrow choke points, you can systematically eliminate the undead one by one using quiet weapons like knives, conserving precious ammunition. This stealthy approach keeps the noise levels down and prevents additional hordes from joining the fray. Human factions, such as biker gangs and cultists, present a much greater threat. They utilize ranged weapons, take cover, and attempt to flank your positions. Against these enemies, stealth is rarely an option, and loud, aggressive tactics are required. Molotov cocktails are exceptionally powerful tools for flushing human targets out of cover and dealing area-of-effect damage.

<Callout type="info" title="Combat Distinctions">
Use quiet melee weapons like knives against isolated zombies to avoid alerting the entire map. Save noisy firearms and explosive Molotovs for heavily armed human factions.
</Callout>
