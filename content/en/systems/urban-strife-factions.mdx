<Section icon={Target} title="Strategic Survival and Faction Conflict Mechanics">

The core conflict in Urban Strife revolves around a hard strategic limitation: only one faction can remain in the suburbs by the time the horde descends. According to developer insights shared on the [Steam Community Forums](https://steamcommunity.com/app/710230/discussions/0/594009125553222539/), this design choice is rooted in the history of the Urban Shelter. Years prior, a massive food riot nearly wiped out the shelter after they allowed too many conflicting groups inside their walls. Consequently, the paranoid residents will not risk letting rival factions co-exist within the safety of the perimeter.

This means you are forced to make a definitive choice. Siding with one faction inevitably requires you to initiate the departure or dismantling of the other two. Your decisions will trigger specific narrative events, altering your standing and changing how you prepare your defenses.

<Section icon={Swords} title="Walkthrough of the Faction Departure Process">

To secure an alliance and prepare for the zombie horde, you must systematically manage the presence of rival **urban strife factions**. This process involves resolving local threats, completing specific alignment quests, and managing the political fallout within your own shelter.

Because the residents of the Urban Shelter refuse to harbor multiple competing groups, you must serve as the catalyst for the other factions' departure. This can be achieved through covert sabotage, direct combat, or by clearing out the specific zombie threats that keep those factions anchored to their current territories.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Establish Initial Contact">
    Locate the representatives of each faction in the suburbs. Complete their introductory tasks, such as investigating the local Quarantine Zone for the military or delivering supplies to the bikers.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Acquire Local Intelligence">
    Speak with the Mayor and key NPCs at the Urban Shelter to understand the community's voting stance. Tune in to the military radio to track the movement of the approaching horde.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Initiate Sabotage or Relocation">
    Begin the process of displacing rival factions. For example, if you side with the Army, you must either destroy the zombies trapping them in their garrison or sabotage the Bikers' moonshining operations to force them to pack up.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Trigger the Final Shelter Vote">
    Once only your chosen ally remains viable in the suburbs, present your alliance proposal to the Shelter council. This secures their cooperation and prepares your combined forces for the defensive siege.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The table below outlines the specific triggers required to make rival factions leave the suburban sector:

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Blueprint Progression and Base Upgrades">

Base building is deeply intertwined with the faction system. Your shelter serves as a central hub for crafting, recovery, and tactical planning. To build advanced facilities like the specialized Workshop, high-yield Gardens, or reinforced Barracks, you must secure blueprints that are held exclusively by specific factions.

These blueprints are not merely passive designs; they represent the specialized knowledge, rare materials, and skilled labor that only certain factions possess. If you do not maintain a high enough reputation with a group, they will withhold these critical upgrades, forcing you to rely on inferior, resource-intensive alternatives.
